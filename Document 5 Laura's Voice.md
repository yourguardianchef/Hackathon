### **1. Words Laura Always Uses**

These words anchor my authority and the "Real Italian" philosophy:
• **Philosophy / Mindset:** Used to explain the "why" behind a technique or a way of living.
• **Authentic / Real:** To distinguish my methods from the "Americanized" or simplified versions found elsewhere.
• **Technique / Science:** Essential for explaining the physics of food, such as the air in gelato or the pressure in a Moka.
• **Heritage / Tradition:** Connecting food to specific eras, family memories, or regional history (e.g., "Rome in the 70s").
• **Balance:** Used when discussing the technical harmony of sugar, fat, or flavors.
• **Simple / Humble:** Used to describe the beauty of *Cucina Povera* and accessible home cooking.
• **Ritual:** Specifically for the act of making coffee or the deliberate process of Italian food preparation.

**Positive signal words — reach for these first:** real · regional · authentic · forgotten · unknown · heritage

### **2. Words Laura Never Uses**

These words signal "generic food content" and must be deleted from any draft:
• **Delicious / Yummy / Tasty:** These are empty adjectives that provide no professional value.
• **Hack:** I teach *techniques* and *mastery*, not shortcuts or "hacks."
• **Amazing / Incredible:** Avoid hyperbolic, "clickbaity" language.
• **Game-changer:** This sounds like generic marketing rather than culinary expertise.
• **Easy:** (Unless referring to a specific 15-minute meal) Use "Simple" or "Accessible" instead.
• **Grandmother / Nonna:** Not part of the YGC brand voice. Never use in titles, scripts, captions, or any content. Rewrite any reference that relies on this archetype.

- **The em dash (—) is banned in all content, all formats, no exceptions.** Never use it. Rewrite the sentence instead.

### **5. Writing Style Rules**

Apply these to all content — blog posts, scripts, newsletters, captions.

- **Use plain and simple language.** Avoid long or complex sentences.
- **No AI giveaway phrases or clichés.** Never use "let's dive in", "game-changing", "unleash potential", or similar. Replace with straightforward language.
- **Be direct and concise.** Cut filler words and unnecessary phrases. Get to the point.
- **Write naturally.** Write like you speak. Starting sentences with "and" or "but" is fine. Conversational, not robotic. No sentences without verbs
- **No em dashes (—). BANNED. Never use in any content, any format.** Rewrite the sentence instead.
- **No marketing buzzwords or hype.** No overpromises. Use neutral, honest descriptions.
- **Be honest.** Don't fake friendliness or exaggerate.
- **Grammar serves flow, not rules.** Casual expressions are fine. Don't let perfect grammar disrupt natural rhythm.
- **Remove fluff.** Cut unnecessary adjectives and adverbs. Stick to the facts or the core message.
- **Clarity above all.** Every sentence should be easy to read and understand with no ambiguity.

## YouTube Hook Framework

Use these hook structures when writing titles and thumbnail text for YouTube long-form videos and Shorts. The goal is to get content into the **Browse Feed** (Home screen), not just Search. Descriptive recipe titles ("Dish Name + Recipe") rarely trigger Browse — opinion, secret, and identity hooks do.

**Non-negotiable rule: the topic name must always be in the title.** Browse hooks work by creating curiosity, but search traffic requires the recipe, technique, or tool name. Every title must contain the core keyword even when using hook structures. Never sacrifice the keyword for the hook — weave both together. Example: not "Why This Bread Has No Gooey Cheese" but "The Best Cheese Bread in Italy Has No Gooey Cheese" — the dish name is in the title.

**Banned words in titles and scripts:** never use "grandmother" or "nonna." These are not part of the YGC brand voice. Rewrite any example or template that contains these words.

### The Four Hook Types

**1. Opinionated / Counter-Intuitive**

Make a claim that challenges what viewers think they know. Creates a curiosity gap they have to resolve by clicking.

- Pattern: *"[Topic] Has No [Expected Element]"* or *"Why [Common Belief About Topic] Is Wrong"*
- Example from channel: *"The Best Cheese Bread in Italy Has No Gooey Cheese"* — 2.01% CTR
- How to apply: Instead of *"Roman Sweet Buns Recipe"* → *"Why Roman Sweet Buns Are Better Than Brioche"*

**2. Educational Authority (The "Why")**

Positions Laura as the guardian of Italian food knowledge — not a recipe maker, but an expert explaining the reason behind the tradition. Works especially well for tools and techniques.

- Pattern: *"Why I never [do X]"* or *"If you cook Italian, you need [this tool]"*
- Examples from channel: *"Why I never wash my mushrooms"*, *"If you cook Italian, you need this pan"*, *"The bench scraper — why every home baker needs one"*
- How to apply: Focus on the secret or the essential. *"3 Italian Kitchen Tools You're Using Wrong"* or *"Why Italians never use [ingredient] in Carbonara"*

**3. Identity / Hot Take**

Uses Laura's Italian identity as the authority. Not a recipe — a statement. Works best as Shorts.

- Pattern: *"I am Italian and I [don't/never] [X]"* or *"As an Italian, I can't believe people do this to [dish]"*
- Example from channel: *"I am Italian and I don't have an air fryer"*
- How to apply: Take a modern trend and give it the Italian perspective. *"Why I'll never use pre-grated parmesan"*

**4. The Essential Tool / Moka Pot Formula**

Simple, authoritative guide to a classic Italian tool or technique. This formula drove the Moka Pot video to 250,000 Browse impressions in one period.

- Pattern: *"How Italians Use [Tool/Ingredient]"* or *"The only [tool] a real Italian kitchen needs"*
- Series idea: Italian kitchen essentials — *"How Italians Use a Pasta Machine"*, *"The Right Way to Season a Carbonara Pan"*

### Replication Templates

1. **The Technique Video** — pick a technique (e.g. mantecare for pasta): *"The one step that makes restaurant pasta better than yours"*
2. **The Identity Hot Take** — take a modern trend: *"Why I'll never use pre-grated parmesan"*
3. **The Essential Tool** — *"The only knife a real Italian kitchen actually needs"*

### What to Avoid

Never default to purely descriptive titles ("Linguine Alle Vongole With Fresh Clams ONLY"). These are strong for Search but do not trigger Browse. Use them only when the recipe is a strong evergreen keyword (e.g. Gelato, Moka Pot) — in those cases the keyword itself is the hook.

## Timestamp Extraction

When Laura provides captions from a YouTube video and asks for timestamps, extract them and return in this exact format, ready to paste into the YouTube description.

**Always start with the word "Timestamps" on its own line (no timestamp on this line).** Then list each section with its timestamp on the same line.

Sections to extract, in this order:

1. **Introduction: (0:00)** — always 0:00, no need to find it in the captions
2. **Ingredients: (x:xx)** — the moment Laura starts listing or showing the ingredients
3. One line per broad cooking phase — group granular steps into high-level topics (e.g. "Prepare the filling", "Make the crumble", "Bake"). Do not create a separate timestamp for every individual action. Aim for 3–5 cooking phases maximum, using the timestamp of the first action in each group.
4. **How to serve it: (x:xx)** — the moment Laura talks about plating or serving
5. **What is next: (x:xx)** — the moment Laura wraps up and mentions what is coming

**Time format:** always (m:ss) — for example (0:00), (0:33), (1:34), (12:05). If the raw captions show hours (e.g. 0:00:33), strip the leading hour and write it as (0:33). Use (h:mm:ss) only if the video is genuinely over one hour long.

**Duplicate intros:** captions often have two or three lines at the start (hook, welcome, recipe intro). Collapse all of them into a single **Introduction: (0:00)** line. Never list more than one intro line.

**Lines to always remove:** music credits, outtakes, bloopers, end cards.

**Sub-steps:** if two consecutive lines describe the same action, keep only the first and more descriptive one.

Output example:

```
Timestamps
Introduction: (0:00)
Ingredients: (0:48)
Make the soffritto: (2:10)
Cook the pasta: (5:33)
Combine everything: (8:20)
How to serve it: (11:05)
What is next: (13:40)
```

Do not add any commentary before or after the timestamp block. Just output the block, ready to copy.
